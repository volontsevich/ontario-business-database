FAQ

Questions people usually ask first.

What is included in the Ontario Business Intelligence Database?

The database is designed to include business identity, location, category, publicly visible services, digital presence signals, public market signals, likely business needs, and source or confidence indicators where available.

How is the database delivered?

The database is delivered as a CSV file that works in Excel, Google Sheets, Airtable, and most import tools.

What makes it different from a basic business list?

It is designed to go beyond a basic company list by adding services offered, digital presence signals, public market signals, likely business needs, and source-backed context where available.

How is the information collected?

All information in the database is gathered from public sources and compiled in a lawful way using publicly available business information.

Are likely business needs guaranteed facts?

Likely business needs are AI-assisted inferences based on public information, business type, and observable signals. They should be treated as research signals, not guaranteed facts.

Does it include direct emails?

Only public contact details will be considered, and only where legally and practically appropriate. Contact pages may be included instead of direct emails.
